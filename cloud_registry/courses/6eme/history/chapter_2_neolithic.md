# Chapter 2 — The Neolithic Revolution (6th Grade)

## Objective
Understand the transition from a nomadic lifestyle to a sedentary one.

## Key Notion to Remember
The **Neolithic** (New Stone Age) started around 10,000 BC. This is when humans became **sedentary** (they stayed in one place).

Major changes:
1. **Agriculture**: Planting wheat, barley, and peas.
2. **Domestication**: Raising animals (sheep, goats, cattle).
3. **Villages**: Building permanent houses.

## Simple Explanation
Instead of searching for food every day, humans learned how to produce it. Because they had fields and animals to look after, they built the first villages. This is the birth of our modern lifestyle.

## Summary
- Sedentarization: Living in one place.
- Invention of farming and animal breeding.
- Birth of the first permanent human settlements.
