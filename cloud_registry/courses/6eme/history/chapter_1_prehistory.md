# Chapter 1 — Prehistory: The Beginnings of Humanity (6th Grade)

## Objective
Understand the origins of humanity and the lifestyle of the first humans (Paleolithic).

## Key Notion to Remember
**Prehistory** begins with the appearance of the first humans around 2.8 million years ago and ends with the invention of writing (around 3,300 BC).

The **Paleolithic** (Old Stone Age):
- Humans were **nomads** (they moved to follow food).
- They lived from hunting, fishing, and gathering.
- They mastered **fire** (around 400,000 years ago).

## Simple Explanation
Early humans were like travelers. They didn't have houses; they lived in temporary camps or caves. Fire was their most important tool: it provided heat, light, and protection from animals.

## Summary
- Prehistory is the longest period in human history.
- Early humans were nomads and hunter-gatherers.
- Fire transformed their way of life.
