# Chapter 1 — Simple Fractions (6th Grade)

## Objective
Understand the concept of a fraction as a part of a whole and represent it visually.

## Key Notion to Remember
A fraction represents a division of a whole into **equal parts**.
It is written as: **Numerator / Denominator**

1. **Denominator** (bottom number): The total number of equal parts in the whole.
2. **Numerator** (top number): The number of parts we are considering or "taking".

## Simple Explanation
Imagine a pizza cut into 4 equal slices.
- If you eat 1 slice, you have eaten **1/4** of the pizza.
- The "4" tells us there were 4 slices in total.
- The "1" tells us you took one slice.

## Examples
- **1/2**: One half (The pizza is cut in 2, you take 1).
- **3/4**: Three quarters (The pizza is cut in 4, you take 3).
- **4/4**: The whole pizza (You took all the parts).

## Summary
- A fraction is a share of a whole.
- The parts must be **perfectly equal**.
- If the Numerator = Denominator, the fraction is equal to **1** (the whole).
