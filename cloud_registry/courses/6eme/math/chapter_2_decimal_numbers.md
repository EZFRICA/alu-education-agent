# Chapter 2 — Decimal Numbers (6th Grade)

## Objective
Understand place value and how to perform basic operations with decimals.

## Key Notion to Remember
A **decimal number** has two parts separated by a decimal point:
1. **Whole part** (on the left).
2. **Decimal part** (on the right).

## Simple Explanation
Think of money: $1.50
- The "1" is the whole dollar.
- The "50" represents the cents (part of a dollar).

Place values:
- 0.1 = Tenths
- 0.01 = Hundredths
- 0.001 = Thousandths

## Examples
- 12.34: 12 is the whole part, 3 is the tenths, 4 is the hundredths.
- Addition: Always line up the decimal points!
  1.5 + 2.25 = 3.75

## Summary
- The decimal point separates wholes from parts.
- Zeros at the very end of the decimal part don't change the value (1.5 = 1.50).
- Lining up the point is the secret to successful calculations.
