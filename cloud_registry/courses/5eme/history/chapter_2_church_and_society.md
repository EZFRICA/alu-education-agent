# Chapter 2 — The Church and Society in the Middle Ages (5th Grade)

## Objective
Understand the role of the Catholic Church in daily life and its power over medieval society.

## Key Notion to Remember
In the Middle Ages, the Church was omnipresent. It organized time (bells), life (baptism, marriage), and even death.

Society was divided into three orders:
1. **Those who pray** (The Clergy): Monks, priests, bishops. Their role is to save souls.
2. **Those who fight** (The Nobility): Knights, lords. Their role is to protect.
3. **Those who work** (The Peasants): They feed everyone.

## Simple Explanation
The Church was the most powerful institution. Everyone believed in God and feared Hell. To go to Heaven, one had to respect the Church's rules and give money (the tithe).

## Keywords
- **The Clergy**: All members of the Church.
- **Cathedral**: A large church where a bishop resides.
- **Excommunication**: Being excluded from the Church (a very serious punishment).

## Summary
- The Church structures the life of every individual.
- It is a very wealthy and powerful institution.
- Faith in God is the core of medieval identity.
