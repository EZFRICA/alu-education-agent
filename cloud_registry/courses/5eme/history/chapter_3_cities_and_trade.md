# Chapter 3 — Cities and Trade in the Middle Ages (5th Grade)

## Objective
Understand why cities developed and how trade transformed society.

## Key Notion to Remember
From the 12th century onwards, we see a **growth of cities**:
- Increased exchanges and trade.
- Development of markets and fairs.
- Growth of crafts and professions.

Important words:
- **Artisan**: A person who makes objects (leather, fabric, metal...).
- **Bourgeois**: Inhabitants of cities who have specific activities (trade, crafts) and sometimes special legal rights.
- **Fair**: A large market that attracts merchants from far away.

## Simple Explanation (Method)
To explain a cause and a consequence:
1. Cause: "Because..."
2. Consequence: "Therefore / This leads to..."

## Example
Cause: Trade routes are developing.  
Consequence: Cities become wealthier and attract more artisans.

## Summary
- Cities grow alongside trade.
- Markets and fairs organize economic exchanges.
- We explain growth using the "cause → consequence" logic.
