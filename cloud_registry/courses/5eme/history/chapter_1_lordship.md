# Chapter 1 — The Lordship and the Manorial System (5th Grade)

## Objective
Understand how land and society were organized in the Middle Ages around the figure of the Lord.

## Key Notion to Remember
A **Lordship** (Seigneurie) is a large territory owned by a Lord, where he exercises power over the people living there (peasants).

The territory is divided into two parts:
1. **The Demesne** (La Réserve): Lands kept by the Lord for himself, worked by peasants.
2. **The Tenures** (Les Tenures): Lands rented to peasants in exchange for taxes (cens) and labor (corvées).

## Simple Explanation
Imagine a huge farm. The owner (the Lord) lives in a fortified castle. He protects the peasants, but in return, they must give him part of their harvest and work on his private fields for free.

## Keywords
- **The Castle**: Symbol of protection and power.
- **The Mill and Oven**: Buildings belonging to the Lord that peasants must pay to use (banalities).
- **The Tithe**: A tax paid to the Church (1/10th of the harvest).

## Summary
- The Lord owns the land and the buildings.
- Peasants work the land and pay taxes.
- In exchange, the Lord provides protection in case of war.
