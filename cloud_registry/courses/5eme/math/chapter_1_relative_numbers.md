# Chapter 1 — Relative Numbers (5th Grade)

## Objective
Understand numbers that can be positive or negative, and how to compare them on a number line.

## Key Notion to Remember
A **relative number** consists of a sign (+ or -) and a value.
- **Positive numbers** (+5, +10) are greater than zero.
- **Negative numbers** (-5, -10) are less than zero.

## Simple Explanation
Think of a thermometer or an elevator:
- **Above zero**: It's getting warmer (+10°C).
- **Below zero**: It's freezing (-10°C).

On a number line:
- The further a negative number is from zero, the **smaller** it is.
- Example: **-10 is smaller than -2**.

## Examples
- At the sea: The beach is at 0m. A bird at 5m is at **+5**. A fish at 3m deep is at **-3**.
- Comparison: **+5 > -10** (Any positive number is greater than any negative number).

## Summary
- Negative numbers are preceded by a minus sign (-).
- Zero is both positive and negative.
- We use relative numbers for temperatures, altitudes, and debts.
